# Lost Pet Finder - Site Planning

## Audience

Parents (helping their child find a lost pet) and strangers who find lost pets.
They are two sides of the same problem, so they count as one connected audience.

## The Problem

When a pet goes missing, the owner has no way to know if a stranger found it, and the stranger has no easy way to contact the owner — because there is no ID tag and no place to connect.

## The Solution

A website that connects both sides:

- Parents can post a missing pet alert
- Strangers can report a found pet
- The site matches the two together so the pet gets home

## Questions and Answers

| # | Question | Answer |
|---|----------|--------|
| 1 | Who loses pets most often? | Kids, because they don't keep a good watch over their pets |
| 2 | What is the first thing a kid does when they lose a pet? | They call a parent |
| 3 | What is the hardest part of finding a lost pet? | The pet can run all over the place, even out of the neighborhood |
| 4 | What stops a stranger from returning a found pet? | No ID tag on the pet |
| 5 | Who is the site for? | Both — parents post alerts, strangers report found pets |
